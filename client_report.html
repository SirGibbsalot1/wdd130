Client report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

Cooking at home is something that I enjoy, so it was easy for me to type a few paragraphs about why I do and some tips for others. I decided to just use images that illustrate the steps. Most of my website was instructional, so I took photos of those steps or had AI generate them for me.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

We mostly talked in class and over text, with occasional emails. Email was mostly to send images and other content. We didn't really have any issues that I'm aware of.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Home page looks pretty good, but as of 7/13/26 it's not all the way finished. I like it so far! It's pretty simple, but honestly my wireframe was pretty minimal, so he's working with what I gave him.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

I would definitely put more time into my wireframe and planning the layout of the page. It makes it a lot easier to develop the page when you know what your client wants.

Website I built for Calin (and his sister)

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

Styling the header and footer in CSS took me a very long time. I used AI a little to help, but it actually wasn't very good at figuring out and correcting issues. I was able to get it to look like the wireframe eventually, though. A lot of trial and error.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

There were a lot of images as it was a website to showcase his sister's artwork, and it was good to learn more about sizing and scaling. It was nice to feel like I understood CSS by the end of it, which made making it mobile friendly go a lot smoother than I was afraid.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I would probably use more flex and block display type over grid, especially in the header and footer. I would hopefully be more efficient with my HTML structuring as well now that I understand that better.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Definitely. I still don't know much about making pages responsive with JavaScript, but I feel good about my ability to write decent HTML and style it with CSS. I have a solid enough understanding to be able to find what I need if I'm struggling with something.
